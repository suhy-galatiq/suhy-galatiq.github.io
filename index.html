Embroidery Compatibility

Best for embroidery (flat, smooth fabric surfaces): Peter Millar Perth, TravisMathew Heater, Marine Layer Sport, all vests (chest area), all cotton tees (Reigning Champ, Lady White Co., Buck Mason).
Requires care (textured or technical fabrics): Arc'teryx Delta (use topping/backing), lululemon Metal Vent Tech (needs stabilizer), lululemon Engineered Warmth (use proper backing), Cuts Clothing (use cut-away stabilizer).
YETI products: Laser engraving only (not embroidery). Permanent, dishwasher-safe, appears as silver/metallic on colored DuraCoat surfaces.

Left Chest Logo Specifics

  • Typical stitch count: 5,000–8,000 stitches for a standard left-chest corporate logo
  • Per-stitch pricing: $1.00–$2.70 per 1,000 stitches
  • Left chest cost: ~$6–$15/piece for a simple logo; $15–$30 for complex/larger designs
  • A clean Galatiq wordmark at ~5,000–6,000 stitches would likely fall $7–$12/piece on orders of 10–25

Setup / Digitizing Fees

  • Digitizing fee (one-time): $20–$75 typical; $35–$75 industry average
  • Setup fee: $0–$50 per order (many shops waive for 25+)
  • Digitizing is a ONE-TIME cost. Once the Galatiq logo is digitized, you reuse it forever.
For Galatiq's likely order size (10–25 pieces):

Small runs (under 24 pieces): Traditional screen printing isn't cost-effective due to screen setup ($25–$50 per color). Use DTG (Direct to Garment) or DTF (Direct to Film) instead — several Austin shops offer this with no minimums.

Embroidery vs. Screen Printing

Embroidery
  • Premium, raised 3D texture
  • High-end, executive perception
  • Best for quarter zips, vests, jackets
  • Exceptional durability — won't crack/peel
  • Industry standard for corporate apparel
  • Safe on technical fabrics
Screen Printing
  • Flat, 2D graphic
  • Casual, promotional perception
  • Best for t-shirts, casual hoodies
  • Good durability, but can crack/peel
  • More casual/event-oriented
  • Better on flat/thin cotton
Recommendation: Use embroidery for premium pieces (quarter zips, vests, hoodies). Consider DTF/DTG for t-shirts if you want a large or full-color graphic. Peter Millar, lululemon, Arc'teryx, and Patagonia all use embroidery for corporate customization.
